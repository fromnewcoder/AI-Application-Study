What is PII?

Personally Identifiable Information (PII) is any data that can be used to identify a specific individual, either directly or in combination with other information.

Direct identifiers — uniquely identify a person on their own:

Full name SSN / NI number Passport number Credit card number Biometric data

Quasi-identifiers — risky when combined (k-anonymity attack):

ZIP code Date of birth Gender Employer IP address

Latanya Sweeney's 2000 study found that 87% of Americans can be uniquely identified by just ZIP + DOB + gender.
